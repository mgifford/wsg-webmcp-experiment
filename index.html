WSG WebMCP Experiment

Why WebMCP?

Web Sustainability Guidelines are currently published primarily as a document intended for human readers. This experiment explores whether WebMCP can expose sustainability guidance as structured tools that can be discovered and used directly by AI assistants, auditing tools, procurement systems, and development environments.

The goal is not to replace standards with AI, but to make standards easier for AI systems to understand and apply accurately.

What happens when WebMCP is available?


  

    When WebMCP is available, this page exposes selected WSG functions as structured
    tools that can be discovered and called by compatible AI agents or browser tooling.
    The visible search form still works in any browser, but WebMCP adds a machine-facing
    interface.
  


  

    For example, instead of an AI assistant scraping this page or guessing from training
    data, it could call:
  


  
wsg.search({ "query": "accessibility" })


  

    The page can then return current WSG data from guidelines.json, including
    matching guidelines, criteria, tags, benefits, and resources.
  


  

    In practical terms, this could allow an AI assistant to help a team draft audit
    questions, identify relevant sustainability criteria, find supporting resources,
    or prepare draft procurement language while staying grounded in the published WSG data.
  


  

    WebMCP does not certify conformance. It makes the WSG more discoverable and usable
    by AI-assisted workflows.
